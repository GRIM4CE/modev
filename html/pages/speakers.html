Michael Madon

Ataata
Human Error and its Role in Security Breaches

It is well known among industry practitioners that security breaches cost companies over $500 billion last year; less well known is the statistic that 95% of those breaches were due to human error. While most employees are not actively seeking to cause their companies harm, few are aware of the significant costs their basic mistakes often create for their organizations. This talk will explore the many problems facing companies and CISOs due to simple human error, recent, high viz examples, and potential solutions.

Chuck Dudley

Stelligent Systems, LLC
Continuous Security: Securing Applications through Continuous Delivery

Continuous Security is the implementation of security best practices and validation through the execution of automated, repeatable code and configuration orchestrated by the Continuous Delivery pipeline. By utilizing automation to implement and test security policies, we simplify the process of demonstrating controls and compliance, documenting policy and allowing for repeatability.

In this talk we cover the stages of a typical Continuous Delivery pipeline and the security and compliance validation that can be inject in each for rapid security feedback and enforcement of security standards.
