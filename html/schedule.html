We will offer several curated tracks to choose from, with topics such as:

Track 1: Building The Dream Team

Getting more women on your technical team (25 min)with Christina Cyr, CEO + Founder of dTOOR

Many companies in Seattle are thankfully eager to find women for their technical teams. However, few are successful. dTOOR is fortunate to have not just one but two female founders, and 70% of its contract work force has been female. Christina Cyr, CEO and Founder of dTOOR discusses strategies she has used to find qualified female contractors for dTOOR's technical work including navigating women in tech events, some of which have their own restrictions for very good reasons. No stranger to leading large teams of women, Christina's last position involved leading a team of no less than 156 women.

All Aboard! Getting Everyone in the Same Boat When Hiring Bootcamp Graduates & Non-Traditional Developers (55 min)with Liz Rush, Independent Devloper + Evangelist

With more and more coding bootcamps graduating all types of developers, we have a growing influx of new engineers to integrate into our workplaces. You're sold on hiring non-traditional developers but now that you've hired someone, it can be tempting to throw your new developer into the deep end of your code base and get back to running your team. How do you ensure that these novice software engineers are productive, happy team members without wasting time or talent? We'll go over strategies for managers, lead developers, and mentors on how to cultivate your new hire and grow a culture that ensures everyone on the team thrives. With experiences pulled from managers of non-traditional developers as well as the bootcamp grads themselves, you'll leave this session with a deeper understanding of the challenges and risks of onboarding bootcamp graduates and the tools to help you manage or mentor your new team member.

The Whiteboard Interview (25 min)with Brook Riggio, VP of Education + Cofounder of Code Fellows

Do you love it or hate it? Interviewers debate it, applicants fear it, but the whiteboard interview won’t go away. Let’s see how to make the most of it by digging in to whiteboardings strengths and weaknesses. We’ll discuss practical tips for growing teams on how to use whiteboard interviews equitably & effectively, and for interviewees on how to get over the fear, and courageously face a code challenge, marker in hand.

Track 2: Career Navigation

The Agile Developer: How Agile Methodologies Have Changed the Ways Developers Work Inside Organizations (25 min)with Dan DiCamillo, Lean, Agile, + DevOps Coach at Getty Images

In this workshop we’ll cover the key areas that makes agile development different, to help you see if it’s for you, ace your interview, and make a great impression with your team

Legal Essentials for Developers (25 min)with Charlene Angeles, Of Counsel Attorney at InVigor Law Group

A legal primer for developers, the talk will focus on basic legal issues including doing business as an entity, working with business partners, essential contracts, protecting intellectual property, and non-compete agreements.

Relating to Developers for Fun and Profit (25 min)with Greg Bulmash, Technical Evangelist at Amazon

Are you a good writer? A good speaker? A good teacher? Do you like helping people? Do you like building and supporting communities? Greg Bulmash, a technical evangelist at Amazon and leader of Seattle CoderDojo, will talk about the growing field of Developer Relations: what it is, what kinds of job opportunities there are, and how you can position yourself for a move into it.

Interviewing Tips - Ask Me Anything (25 min) with Tony Wilson, Director of Software Engineering at Capital One Investing

Interviewing never seems to get any easier - even when you have gone on more interviews than you can count. Ask advice and get insight on ways you can improve your chances at the interview.

Track 3: Parallel Skills To Boost Your Coding

Your Mouse is Stealing Your Time (55 min)with Jeremy Foster, Developer Evangelist at Microsoft

Tapping keys. It's the one thing every developer on every platform in every language does thousands of times per day, and the general rule is that fewer taps are better. This one cross-cutting concern is ripe with opportunities to improve your productivity and efficiency. In this session, we'll look at… - how to move more of your tasks to the command line and away from the mouse - how to identify and automate especially time sucking tasks - how to commit keyboard shortcuts to memory - how to start using keyboard shortcuts where you couldn't before - how to use the keyboard on tasks you might not today such as managing github repos, browsing the operating system UI, fetching data from the web, and even moving your mouse cursor - how to improve your typing speed on code specifically

Fun in Gerrit Code Review" (55 min)with Emiko Hori, Software Engineering Manager at CommerceHub

Gerrit is a free, web-based team collaboration code review tool which was developed at Google (https://code.google.com/p/gerrit/ ). Adopting Google Project Gerrit is fun! In this presentation, you'll get a chance to learn the basics of Gerrit: what is Gerrit; how we integrate with Git as well as Continuous Integration tools. We will explore topics including how to adopt Gerrit seamlessly, how to collaborate among teams on code review process, how to navigate Gerrit UI, how to troubleshoot, how to administer Gerrit, and so on. You will learn a benefit of this valuable code review tool as a part of database/application development process improvement.

3:00 - 4:00pm — Ted-Style Lightning talks

Featuring:

Don't Learn a New Language (15 min)with Brenden West, Founder + CTO of Brisk Software

After learning a first programming language, new software developers often ask - "what language should I learn next?" - thinking this approach will improve their chances of landing a job. More senior developers will be happy to suggest other (favorite) languages to learn. This talk will cover why that advice is bad and what junior developers should do instead.

Recipes for Success (15 min)with Jason Yee, Technical Writer + Evangelist at Datadog

Although the jobs of software engineer and chef are vastly different, there are a lot of interesting similarities. I’ll share a few valuable lessons on technique, tools, burnout and community that I learned while cooking in some of the world’s best restaurants and how they apply to working in tech.

How to Be a SMART Ally (10 min)with Joanna Power, Software Engineer at 98point6

This short talk is aimed at technology professionals who understand that the tech industry has a diversity problem but don't know what they can personally do to make things better. The talk will not attempt to convince anyone that there is a lack of diversity in tech, that this lack is not due to a lack of talent among diverse candidates, or that the tech industry will be stronger when its diversity increases. It will instead present five specific actions that we can all take in our everyday lives to make the technology industry more welcoming to underrepresented minorities. It will also provide links to resources that allies may find helpful.
